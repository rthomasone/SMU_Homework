Summary

Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

There does not appear to be a strong correlation between the portion of the population without healthcare and poverty. States with the highest percentages in poverty, such as New Mexico and Mississippi, do not have the largest number of residents without healthcare. Similarly, approximately 25% of Texas' population is without healthcare but it is not the state with the highest level of poverty.

Median age across the states was clustered between 36 and 40. There is a not strong correlation between those without healthcare and the median age per state.
